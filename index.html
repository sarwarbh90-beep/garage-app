🚗 Essential Car Care Tips

🔧 Engine Health

Never ignore a "Check Engine" light. Changing your engine oil every 5,000km to 10,000km prevents expensive engine overhaul costs.

⚙️ Gearbox & Transmission

If you feel a "jerk" while shifting, check your transmission fluid immediately. Clean gear oil extends the life of your gearbox.

❄️ AC Performance

In Bahrain's heat, clean your cabin air filter every 6 months to ensure maximum cooling and better air quality inside the car.

🛑 Brake Safety

Squeaking noises when braking usually mean your brake pads are worn out. Replace them early to avoid damaging the brake discs.

🛞 Tire Pressure

Check tire pressure monthly. Properly inflated tires improve fuel efficiency and provide better grip on Salmabad roads.

⚙️ Car Sensor & Diagnostic Guide

🔴 Oxygen Sensor (O2 Sensor)

Is your car consuming too much fuel? A faulty O2 sensor drops your mileage and can cause black smoke. Replacing it early saves you money on petrol.

🟡 MAF Sensor (Mass Air Flow)

If your engine vibrates while idling or stalls suddenly, your MAF sensor might be dirty. Get it professionally cleaned to restore engine smoothness.

🟢 ABS & Speed Sensor

If the ABS light stays on, your wheel speed sensors might be failing. For safe braking and traction on Salmabad roads, get this checked immediately.

🔵 Coolant Temp Sensor

This sensor protects your engine from overheating. If your temperature gauge is high, stop the car and let our experts check the sensor and cooling system.

🟠 Crankshaft Position Sensor

Engine won't start even if it cranks? This sensor controls engine timing. A faulty sensor will prevent your car from starting entirely.

🛡️ Car Care & Safety Tips

Expert advice for driving in the Bahrain heat

The Bahrain heat puts extreme stress on your radiator. Always check your coolant levels and look for leaks to prevent your engine from overheating.
High temperatures cause battery fluid to evaporate faster. If your car takes longer to start, bring it to Black Gold Auto for a free battery health check.
Hot asphalt can increase tire pressure and cause blowouts. Check your tires every morning and ensure they have enough tread depth for safe braking.
